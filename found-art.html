The Case for Original Art

Owning art is one
of the most human
things you can do.

It’s not about investment returns or impressing anyone. It’s about surrounding yourself with things made by other humans, for no practical reason at all — just feeling and expression.

01

It directly sustains an artist’s life

When you buy an original directly from an artist, you’re paying their rent, their materials, their next year of making. Galleries take 50%. Platforms take 30–40%. Buying direct means close to 100% reaches the person who made it.

02

A 1-of-1 is genuinely irreplaceable

No one else in the world owns this specific object. The canvas with those marks, in that light, made on that particular day. You’re not buying access to an image. You’re acquiring the thing itself.

03

It tells the world who you are

Art on your wall is autobiography. What you find beautiful, what unsettles you, what you want to wake up to. A home full of original art is a home that has something to say — about you, not a decorator’s sense of safety.

04

Now is genuinely the right moment

The artists priced at $500–$2,000 today are the ones with gallery shows in five years. The windows at these prices close. The best time to start is before the rest of the room knows the name.

05

Living with art changes you

Not metaphorically — literally. A painting you see every morning for a year does something to how you look at the world. You start noticing light differently. Art teaches attention.

06

The financial side is real, not mythical

Not every piece appreciates dramatically. But original works by artists whose careers are building have a track record. Buying at the right moment in a career isn’t speculating — it’s collecting with your eyes open.
