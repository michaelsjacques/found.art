Today's Lesson
What Does "Pricing Discipline" Actually Mean?
When an artist raises prices incrementally — typically 20–40% between series — and sells through without leaving works unsold, that's pricing discipline. It signals demand is real, not manufactured. It means the artist and their community are aligned. Collectors who bought Series I are rewarded. The work gains legitimacy not from institutional validation but from market behaviour.

Contrast this with an artist who spikes prices speculatively — often as a response to attention rather than proven sell-through. These works are frequently resold at a discount, depressing secondary market trust.

We track pricing discipline as one of five signals in our Conviction Score. A score above 90 in this dimension — like Amara Osei's 96 — is a meaningful indicator.
