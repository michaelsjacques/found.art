Why collect
original art?

01

It lives with you

A print is decoration. An original is a relationship. Every morning you see it differently — because you are different. Art that was made by a human hand has a presence that reproductions don't.

02

Emerging artists are affordable

The most interesting work in the market right now costs between $500 and $5,000. That's where careers are being made and where collectors with taste — not just capital — win.

03

It tends to hold value

Art bought from the right emerging artists at the right moment has a track record of appreciating. That's not the primary reason to collect — but it's not a reason against it either.

04

You support a person

When you buy an original, you're not paying a corporation's margin. You're paying an artist's rent, studio costs, and the time they spend making something only they could make.

05

The market rewards early

Collectors who bought Cecily Brown or Jonas Wood at the beginning of their careers paid a fraction of today's prices. That window exists for many artists working right now.

06

It starts a conversation

Good art prompts questions. Who made this? Why? What's it about? The piece on your wall becomes an entry point into a larger world — artists, ideas, movements, history.

“Buy with your eyes, not your ears. Trust what you’re drawn to most.”
